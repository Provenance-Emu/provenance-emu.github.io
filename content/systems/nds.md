Provenance includes Nintendo DS emulation for iPhone, iPad, and Apple TV. The DS had a remarkable dual-screen library — Pokemon Diamond and Pearl, Mario Kart DS, The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass, Chrono Trigger DS, Professor Layton, and hundreds more — and Provenance brings that library to your Apple device.

Available free on the App Store, no jailbreak required. Provenance is fully open-source and community-developed.

## Supported File Formats

- `.nds` — Standard Nintendo DS ROM format

## Features

- **Dual-Screen Display** — Provenance renders both DS screens, with layout options to fit your device's screen
- **Save States** — Save and resume anywhere, independent of the DS's own save system
- **Battery Save Emulation** — In-game saves work as expected, preserving your in-game progress
- **Controller Support** — MFi, Xbox, and PlayStation controllers are supported; touchscreen input handles DS touch-screen interactions
- **Fast Forward** — Speed through cutscenes and dialogue-heavy sections

## iCloud Sync with Provenance Plus

[Provenance Plus]({{< relref "/plus/" >}}) adds iCloud sync for your Nintendo DS save states, keeping your game progress backed up and synchronized across your iPhone, iPad, and Apple TV.

## How to Get Started

1. **Download Provenance** from the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/provenance-app/id1596862805) — it's free.
2. **Add your NDS ROM files** via iTunes File Sharing, the Files app, or Provenance's web import feature.
3. **Start playing** — Provenance detects and displays your Nintendo DS games in your library.

For detailed setup instructions, visit the [Provenance wiki](https://wiki.provenance-emu.com/).
