Supported System

Atari 2600

ROM formats: .a26, .bin, .rom  (Atari 2600 / VCS)

Download Free Learn About Plus

The Atari 2600 — originally sold as the Video Computer System — launched in 1977 and brought arcade gaming into living rooms for the first time. Its library of Pitfall!, Space Invaders, Pac-Man, Asteroids, and Combat defined early home gaming. With Provenance, you can play the entire 2600 catalog on your iPhone, iPad, or Apple TV.

Provenance is free, open-source, and available directly from the App Store. No jailbreak required.

Supported File Formats

Atari 2600 ROM files are small by modern standards. Provenance supports all common formats:

  • .a26 — The dedicated Atari 2600 ROM extension
  • .bin — Generic binary format; the most common format for 2600 dumps
  • .rom — Alternate ROM extension used by some archiving tools

Because Atari 2600 cartridges were so small — most are under 32KB — entire libraries take up a fraction of modern storage.

Features

Playing Atari 2600 games in Provenance gives you access to the full suite of emulator features:

  • Save States — Save at any point; most 2600 games had no native save functionality, making this essential for longer sessions
  • Controller Support — Full MFi, Xbox, and PlayStation controller compatibility; the simple 2600 joystick maps naturally to a single analog stick and button
  • Shader Support — CRT scanline and phosphor filters to recreate the warm, blurry look of playing on a 1970s television
  • Cheat Codes — Code support for supported titles
  • Fast Forward — Accelerate through slower sections
  • On-Screen Controls — Customizable touch overlay for playing without a physical controller

iCloud Sync with Provenance Plus

Provenance Plus adds iCloud sync for your Atari 2600 save states and game library. Your progress and high scores stay consistent whether you’re playing on your iPhone or Apple TV.

How to Get Started

  1. Download Provenance from the App Store — it’s free.
  2. Add your Atari 2600 ROM files to Provenance using iTunes File Sharing, the Files app, or a web server import.
  3. Start playing — your game library appears automatically once files are imported.

For detailed setup instructions, visit the Provenance wiki.
